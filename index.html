What is P4P Ranking ?

Pound for pound is a ranking used in combat sports, such as boxing, wrestling, or mixed martial arts. It is based on who is the most skilled/best boxer if you take out weight differences

8. Manny Pacquiao

He is the first boxer to win the lineal championship in five different weight classes, won 12 major world titles in eight different weight divisions. Most recently he beat Keith Thurman convicingly to win the WBA (Super) welterweight championship. Pacquiao powers are definetely diminishing from the levels he was once at but he is still one of the best ever.

Golovkin often known by his nickname "GGG" is a two time middleweight world champion. He previously held the unified WBA (Super), WBC, IBF and IBO titles between 2014 and 2018 and was ranked as the world's best boxer, pound for pound, from September 2017 to September 2018 by The Ring magazine. In GGG last fight beat Sergiy Derevyanchenko by a controversial unanimous decision. This led to pundits questioning if GGG was done performing at the highest level. 6. Oleksandr Usyk

Usyk reigned as the undisputed cruiserweight champion from 2018 until he vacated his titles in 2019, being the first cruiserweight in history to hold all four major world championships—the WBA (Super), WBC, IBF and WBO titles. He claimed that status in stunning fashion by becoming the first man to hold the Muhammad Ali Trophy. Since then, he has vacated his belts and cruiserweight division in favour of the heavyweight division. His first clash as a heavyweight was against Chazz Witherspoon where he dispatched of Chazz within 7 rounds but there questions of if he would be able to compete with the divisions top dogs. With a potential matchup with AJ as his manditory seeming more and more distant he will have ample opportunity to prove himself, especially against a dangerous Derek Chisora
